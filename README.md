# Maven-Telecom-Churn-Report-Dashboard
Customer churn analysis dashboard built in Excel using PivotTables, charts, slicers, and KPI cards.

# 📌 Project Overview

This project is an interactive Excel Dashboard built to analyze customer churn for a fictional telecommunications company, Maven Telecom.

The objective of this dashboard is to help business stakeholders identify:

◦ Why customers are churning

◦ Which customer segments are at high risk

◦ Revenue impact of churn

◦ Key recommendations for reducing churn

# 🎯 Business Problem

Customer churn directly affects revenue growth and customer lifetime value. Maven Telecom wanted to understand:

◦ What are the main reasons for churn?

◦ Are high-value customers being lost?

◦ Which plans/services have the highest churn?

◦ What actions can reduce churn?

# 🛠 Excel Technical Stack

◦ Advanced Formulas: Used IF statements and conditional formulas to create custom customer segments and business logic.

◦ Data Transformation: Utilized Power Query to clean, format, and prepare raw telecommunications data for analysis.

◦ Dashboard KPIs: Built KPI cards for key metrics such as Total Revenue, Churn Rate, Joined Customers, and Revenue Lost.

◦ Interactive Filters: Added slicers for Gender, Payment Method, and Age Group to make the dashboard easy to explore.

◦ Charts & Visuals: Created a dashboard layout using Donut Charts, Bar Charts, Stacked Charts, and customer KPI visuals.

# 🚀 Key Business Insights
◦ Financial Impact: Identified a significant $3.68M revenue loss from churned customers.

◦ The "Fiber" Problem: Fiber Optic users exhibit a 70.39% churn rate, indicating service or pricing dissatisfaction.

◦ Contractual Risk: 88.6% of all churned customers were on Month-to-Month plans, representing the highest risk group.

◦ Customer Value: Churned customers paid an average of $73/month—18% more than staying customers—meaning the company is losing its most profitable clients.

◦ Competitor Loss: Over 600 customers left due to better competitor offers and hardware.

# 💡 Recommendations
◦ Encourage month-to-month customers to switch to yearly plans.

◦ Improve Fiber service quality and pricing.

◦ Offer better deals to prevent customers switching to competitors.

◦ Support new customers during the first few months.

◦ Retain high-value customers with rewards and premium support.
